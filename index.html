Fysiverse: Towards Physics-Grounded World Model via Enforcing Causal Physical Consistency

Abstract

World models are promising for embodied intelligence, but existing methods still struggle to maintain physical consistency in generated videos. A practical direction is to combine physics simulation with video generation, yet current pipelines remain limited by unreliable physical parameter estimation and weak physical conditioning. We propose Fysiverse, a physics-grounded world model that addresses these bottlenecks through a unified real-to-sim and sim-to-real pipeline. For the real-to-sim stage, we introduce PGReason, which grounds material parameter estimation by retrieving validated material properties from a retrieval-augmented database and analyzing surface reflectance for friction estimation, providing reliable inputs for physics simulation. For the sim-to-real stage, we present PGConds, which transforms simulated dynamics into instance masks, depth maps, and tracking cues to provide video diffusion models with richer constraints on object interactions, spatial structure, and motion. Extensive quantitative and qualitative experimental results indicate that Fysiverse outperforms existing approaches in both visual fidelity and physical consistency.

Limitation

Since we fuse three conditioning signals that carry rich information, they occasionally interfere with the object's appearance, which leads to noticeable appearance alterations, as shown in Case 1 and Case 2. To stabilize the background and suppress camera jitter, we employ a global tracking map instead of an object-level one. However, this design conflicts with scenarios requiring dynamic background motion. In Case 3, dust particles illuminated by light rays appear frozen.
